Hotel Customer Satisfaction & Sales Analysis

Tools Used

Power BI, Excel

Data Source

Hotel Transaction & Customer Satisfaction Dataset Kaggle

Background

Hotels collect large volumes of transactional and customer feedback data, but without proper analysis, valuable insights remain hidden. This project focuses on transforming hotel transaction data and customer satisfaction ratings into an interactive Power BI dashboard that supports data-driven decision-making. The goal was to understand how customer satisfaction varies across hotels, regions, and customer segments, and how satisfaction impacts sales performance.

Objective

  • Monitor overall customer satisfaction levels
  • Identify top and underperforming hotels and regions
  • Understand customer behavior across segments and memberships
  • Analyze the relationship between customer satisfaction and sales
  • Support strategic decisions to improve guest experience and revenue

Data Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and transformed using Power Query to ensure reliable and accurate insights:

  • Corrected data types for ratings, sales, and customer counts
  • Standardized customer review and category labels
  • Removed inconsistencies and duplicate records
  • Prepared the dataset for analysis and reporting

Data Modeling

A structured data model was implemented in Power BI to support efficient analysis:

  • Central fact table containing transaction and satisfaction data
  • Dimensions for hotel, region, customer segment, and membership
  • DAX measures were created for KPIs such as average rating, positive satisfaction rate, total sales, and customer counts
  • Enabled dynamic filtering and consistent calculations across the report

Key Metrics (DAX-Driven KPIs)

  • Average Customer Rating
  • Positive Satisfaction Rate (4–5 stars)
  • Low Satisfaction Rate (1–2 stars)
  • Total Transactions
  • Total Sales
  • Average Sales per Customer

Project Overview

Key Business Insights

  • Hotels with higher satisfaction ratings generate higher average sales
  • Positive customer reviews strongly align with 4–5 star ratings
  • Membership customers tend to spend more and report better experiences
  • Regional and hotel-level differences reveal clear improvement opportunities

Final Thoughts

This report transformed raw hotel transaction and customer satisfaction data into clear, actionable insights. Key takeaways include:

  • Satisfaction Drives Sales: Hotels with higher customer ratings generated more revenue, showing the strong link between experience and performance.
  • Membership Advantage: Members tended to spend more and reported better satisfaction compared to non-members.
  • Regional & Hotel Insights: Certain regions and hotels outperformed others, highlighting areas for improvement.
  • Customer Reviews: Positive reviews (4–5 stars) correlated with higher sales, while low ratings signaled service gaps.

With these insights, hotel management can prioritize service improvements, enhance guest experiences, and drive revenue growth.
