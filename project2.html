Mental Health & Well-Being Analysis

Tools Used

Power BI, Power Query, Excel

Data Source

Mental Health Survey Dataset

Background

Mental health has become an increasingly important topic in modern workplaces. However, stress, stigma, and lack of awareness often prevent individuals from seeking professional help. This project focuses on transforming mental health survey data into an interactive Power BI dashboard to better understand stress patterns, treatment behavior, and workplace support across different demographics and occupations.

Objective

  • Understand mental health treatment patterns across demographics
  • Identify factors associated with high stress and coping struggles
  • Analyze the impact of workplace conditions on mental well-being
  • Evaluate awareness of mental health care options
  • Support data-driven strategies to improve workplace mental health support

Data Preparation

Data cleaning and transformation were performed using Power Query to ensure accurate and consistent analysis:

  • Standardized Yes/No categorical responses
  • Handled missing values in employment-related fields
  • Renamed columns for clarity and consistency
  • Grouped related mental health indicators for analysis
  • Prepared the dataset for data modeling and reporting

Data Modeling

A structured data model was implemented in Power BI to support scalable and efficient analysis:

  • Central fact table containing mental health survey responses
  • Dimension tables for demographics, employment, and mental health factors
  • Custom date table derived from timestamps for time-based analysis
  • DAX measures created to ensure consistent KPI calculations

Key Metrics (DAX-Driven KPIs)

  • Total Respondents
  • Treatment Rate (%)
  • High Stress Rate (%)
  • Coping Struggles Rate (%)
  • Work Interest Decline (%)
  • Care Options Awareness (%)

Project Overview

Key Analytical Insights

  • Individuals with a family history of mental illness are more likely to seek treatment
  • Higher stress levels strongly correlate with coping struggles and mood swings
  • Longer periods spent indoors are associated with increased stress
  • Many respondents are unsure about available mental health care options
  • Workplace stigma remains a barrier to open mental health discussions

Final Thoughts

This project transformed raw mental health survey data into meaningful, actionable insights that highlight key risk factors and awareness gaps. Key takeaways include:

  • Stress & Coping: High stress is closely linked to coping struggles, mood swings, and reduced work interest.
  • Treatment Behavior: Family history plays a significant role in whether individuals seek mental health treatment.
  • Workplace Awareness: Many employees lack clarity on available mental health care options.
  • Stigma Impact: Reluctance to discuss mental health in professional settings remains a major challenge.

These insights can help organizations design better mental health policies, improve employee well-being, and foster a more supportive workplace culture.
